Japanese Box Office, September 15-16
posted on 2012-09-24 08:30 UTC-8 by Crystalyn Hodgkins
Rurouni Kenshin falls to #3; Himitsu no Akko-chan stays in top 10

Keishi Otomo's live-action film adaptation of Nobuhiro Watsuki's Rurouni Kenshin manga 
fell from No. 2 to No. 3 during its fourth weekend. Takeru Satoh (Kamen Rider Den-O, 
Rookies, Beck) stars in the film's titular role, and Emi Takei (Kyo, Koi o Hajimemasu, 
For Love's Sake/Ai to Makoto) plays the heroine Kaoru Kamiya. Yuu Aoi (Hana and Alice, 
Honey and Clover) plays Megumi Takani, Munetaka Aoki (The Last Message Umizaru, 
live-action The Girl Who Leapt Through Time) plays Sanosuke Sagara, and 
Taketo Tanaka plays Yahiko Myojin. Yosuke Eguchi (Shonan Bakusozoku and Tokyo Love Story) 
as Hajime Saito, Kouji Kikkawa (Let's Dance With Papa) as Jine Udo, and Teruyuki Kagawa 
(From Up On Poppy Hill, 20th Century Boys) as Kanryu Takeda round out the cast. 
The film earned US$2,061,833 on 313 screens for a gross total of US$30,083,065.

The live-action film adaptation of Fujio Akatsuka's shojo comedy manga 
Himitsu no Akko-chan fell from No. 8 to No. 10 during its third weekend. 
In the manga, the title fifth-grader Akko-chan receives a magical compact 
from the mirror spirit. With the compact, Akko-chan can cast spells and transform 
herself into anything or anyone she wishes, and she uses the power to help others. 
In the film's updated romantic fantasy story, Akko-chan (Socrates in Love and 
Ichi's Haruka Ayase) begins as a 22-year-old woman whose life is transformed 
when she is offered a magical compact. Masaki Okada 
(Otomen, Maho Tsukai ni Taisetsu na Koto, Space Bros./Uchu Kyodai) will play the 
love interest, an "elite salaryman" at a cosmetics company. Over the course of the film, 
Akko-chan transforms into a figure skater, an airline cabin attendant, an office lady, 
a female police officer, a motorcycle racer, and more. 
The film earned US$663,465 on 217 screens for a new total of US$5,316,522.

Mamoru Hosoda's Okami Kodomo no Ame to Yuki (Wolf Children) 
film fell from No. 10 to No. 14 during its ninth weekend. 
This is the latest film by the director of Summer Wars and The Girl Who Leapt Through Time. 
The film's story covers 13 years and begins with a 19-year-old college student 
named Hana who encounters and falls in "fairy tale-like" love with a "wolf man." 
After marrying the wolf man, Hana gives birth and raises two 
wolf children — an older sister named Yuki ("snow") who was born on a snowy day, 
and a younger brother named Ame ("rain") who was born on a rainy day. 
When the wolf man suddenly dies, Hana decides to move to a rural town far 
removed from the city. The film earned US$516,881 on 381 screens for 
a new total of US$51,000,621.

The fourth live-action film based on Shuho Sato's (Say Hello to Black Jack, 
The Isle of TOKKOU) Umizaru coast guard manga, Brave Hearts Umizaru, 
fell from No. 11 to No. 15 during its 10th weekend. 
The film adapts the "largest sea disaster" from the original manga: 
the water landing of a jumbo jet. The film earned US$366,744 on 450 screens for a 
gross-to-date of US$91,331,832.

Shinji Aramaki's Starship Troopers: Invasion CG film earned $13 on one screen in 
its ninth weekend at No. 18 for a new total of US$192,567.

The live-action film adaption of Shohei Manabe's Ushijima the Loan Shark 
(Yamikin Ushijima-kun) manga fell from Box Office Mojo's chart in its fourth weekend. 
Gekijoban Pocket Monster Best Wishes! Kyurem Vs Seikenshi Keldeo also fell from 
Box Office Mojo's chart in its 10th weekend.

Box Office Mojo lists Resident Evil: Retribution as the top-grossing film of the 
weekend of September 15-16 earning US$8,650,515, but Kogyo Tsushinsha lists the 
film at No 2.
